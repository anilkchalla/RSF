About the forum

A new scientific community around reductive stress

The Reductive Stress Forum brings together leading scientists from diverse disciplines to advance our collective understanding of reductive stress biology and its implications for human health. This inaugural meeting marks the establishment of a dedicated platform for rigorous scientific exchange on a fundamentally underexplored dimension of redox biology.

Scientific focus

Symposium themes

Reductive stress mechanisms

Cellular and molecular drivers of reductive stress states, including NAD(P)H accumulation, GSH/GSSG imbalance, and antioxidant pathway dysregulation.

Chronic disease intersections

Role of reductive stress in cardiometabolic disorders, neurodegeneration, cancer biology, and aging-related pathologies.

Multi-omics and redox profiling

Proteomics, metabolomics, and transcriptomics approaches for identifying reductive stress signatures in animal models and clinical cohorts.

Molecular adaptations

Protein modifications, signaling pathway rewiring, and gene expression changes that define adaptive and maladaptive responses to reductive stress.

About the founder

Rajasekaran Namakkal-Soorappan, PhD
Associate Professor & Founder, Reductive Stress Forum
Cardiac Aging and Redox Signaling Laboratory
Center for Free Radical Biology
Division of Molecular and Cellular Pathology
Department of Pathology, University of Alabama at Birmingham
Adjunct Faculty, Division of Cardiovascular Medicine, University of Utah
Dr. Namakkal-Soorappan is a leading authority on reductive stress biology, widely recognized for establishing reductive stress as a distinct and clinically significant arm of the redox spectrum. His laboratory investigates how excess reducing equivalents — driven by overactivation of the Nrf2/antioxidant defense axis — cause proteotoxic cardiomyopathy, diastolic dysfunction, impaired myogenesis, and neurodegeneration. His work has fundamentally challenged the assumption that antioxidants are unconditionally beneficial, opening new avenues toward precision redox modulation therapy. He convened the landmark Antioxidants & Redox Signaling forum issue “Reductive Stress: Neglected Science,” which catalyzed field-wide attention to this underexplored biology.
